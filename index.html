Varanasi's ghats are riverfront steps that lead to the Ganges' banks. There are 88 ghats in the city. The majority of the ghats are utilised for bathing and puja ceremonies, while two ghats, Manikarnika and Harishchandra, are solely for cremation.

Most Varanasi ghats were rebuilt in the 18th century under the maratha patronage. The patrons of current ghats are Marathas, Shindes (Scindias), Holkars, Bhonsles, and Peshwes (Peshwas). Maharajas of Benares.

Many ghats have legends or mythologies attached to them, and many ghats are privately held. A famous tourist attraction is a morning boat trip on the Ganges through the ghats.
